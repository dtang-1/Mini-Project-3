Drone Controversy

Civilians flying drones has caused lots of controversy. The government does not want people flying drones that can damage or invade privacy. A $400 remote control quadcopter was landed on White house grounds. Just weeks before a meeting was held and videos of low costing drones firing weapons was shown. A DJ phantom 2 was used, the same model as the one that landed on the White House. Civilians think they should have the right to fly drones but is it really worth the risk?

How Drones Work

Drones, they can be quadcopters or even octocopters. The quadcopters work on a very simple system. Each motor/propeller spinning in the opposite direction from the two motors on either side of it, opposite rotors spin in the same direction. To control pitch and turn by speeding up two motors on one side and slowing down the other two. Altitude is controlled by speeding up and slowing down all motors at the same time. The copters themselves are not aerodynamically stable. They require an on board computer to analyze data from gyroscopes and accelerometers to maintain a stable flight.
