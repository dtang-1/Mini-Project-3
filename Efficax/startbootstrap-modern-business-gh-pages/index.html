Samsung Galaxy s7 vs IPhone 7

          In today’s heated cellular market, companies must engineer new features to keep them from falling behind competitors. Company flagships are often released within the same time-bracket of others. Today we’ll be looking at Samsung’s new titan upgraded from last year’s galaxy line up, the s7 edge and Apple's famous iPhone which has long dominated the market.

Phone Features

The Samsung s7 specs:

  • 5.5 inch SUPER AMOLED.
  • 1440 X 2560 pixels, ~534 ppi.
  • 150.9 X 72.6 X 7.7 mm, 157 grams.
  • Android v6.0 Marshmallow.
  • Curved Edge display.
  • Snapdragon 820, Exynos 8890 Octa.
  • 4GB RAM, 32 / 64 GB, micro SD up to 200 GB.
  • 12 MP primary camera

The iPhone 7 specs:

  • 4.7-inch Retina HD LED-backlit widescree
  • 1,334 x 750 pixels (326 ppi)
  • iOS 10
  • 32, 128 or 256GB
  • A10 Fusion with 64-bit architecture, M10 motion coprocessor
  • 2MP rear, 7MP front
  • 4.87 ounces (138 grams)

Phone Features

          These two products are made to compete against the others directly but in entirely different ways. Both designed to be part of their respective flagship phones' line up. Both are the most expensive of the phones the market. The first difference is in the hardware. The iPhone 7 plus is larger but both have the same screen size at 5.5 inches. Both have distinguishing features, s7 with its edge capabilities and apple with its 3D touch. The stereo speakers on the iPhone are not only louder but more immersive. But the s7 soars with a higher rating of waterproofing at ip68 rating higher than iPhone’s ip67 giving excuse to its weak speakers. In battery life, the s7 has double the battery life of the iPhone. But they burn at the same pace because of the added pixel density on the s7. The edge also pulls ahead with the new Qualcomm Quick Charge 2.0 technology charging at a much higher rate than the iPhone’s lightning port. Their cameras are very similar at 12 megapixels and both phones have amazing color saturation and contrast in the day time. The s7 only has a 1.1 aperture while the iPhone has a 1.7 aperture. The iPhone can capture many more details then the s7 with its telephoto lenses. The s7 makes up with its laser focusing, focusing almost the instant an object is introduced. If you value looks, design, memory expandability and a plethora of features the s7 is the way to go. Otherwise go with the iPhone’s camera, reliability, consistency and smoothness of the iPhones software.

Conclusion

          In conclusion, the Samsung Galaxy s7 emerges victorious. Frankly, it is just a better made phone. It is designed with better features, cheaper and has an audio jack. Clearly, the Samsung Galaxy s7 is better.
